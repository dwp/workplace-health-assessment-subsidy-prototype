Provider terms and conditions

This page explains the terms and conditions for a provider to join the workplace health assessment register. You must agree to these before you can join the register.

Ways of working

General obligations

You agree that you will:

  • work within these terms and conditions
  • follow the law and act honestly and fairly
  • tell DWP of any changes to your information within 7 days of them happening
  • tell SMEs that any other discounts you offer in your service have not been funded by DWP
  • maintain the reputation of the scheme and DWP by giving SMEs correct information
  • not say that you are endorsed by DWP, but if accepted and want to tell people, that you are registered on the scheme
  • not say that you have a relationship of employment, agency, partnership, or joint venture with DWP
  • not say that DWP guarantees the quality of your health assessments
  • not accept any discount codes if DWP pauses or stops the scheme because you will not be paid
  • not charge more than £400 for an assessment through this service

Clinical governance

You agree that:

  • before assessments, you get a referral from the employer to understand their employee’s needs
  • at assessments, the employee will be told about what will be included in the report
  • the employee will be given the option to see the report before it is sent to their employer
  • if the assessment is completed by a trainee, they will be supported by a fully certified healthcare professional
  • all clinical staff are professionally indemnified with a reputable insurance company
  • you have the correct quality assurance, quality risk, management and incident procedures in place which are in line with your professional legal requirements
  • audits of clinical practice are systematic, and you can provide evidence of any actions that have resulted from this
  • If your GMC, HCPC or NMC licence expires, you will be suspended from the provider register until an eligibility reassessment has been carried out by DWP

Complaints procedures and feedback mechanisms

You agree that:

  • you have a complaints procedure
  • you can request feedback from the employer or employees who are part of the scheme
  • you have a fair process that allows either the employer or employees to raise concerns about how services are delivered
  • you understand that DWP is not responsible for any complaints procedures or feedback mechanisms

Complaints procedures and feedback mechanisms

You agree that:

  • you have a complaints procedure
  • you can request feedback from the employer or employees who are part of the scheme
  • you have a fair process that allows either the employer or employees to raise concerns about how services are delivered
  • you understand that DWP is not responsible for any complaints procedures or feedback mechanisms

Data management policies

You agree that you have:

  • a privacy policy that complies with data protection law
  • registration with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO), for example a certificate
  • a GDPR record management policy that complies with data protection law, including safe transportation, access, storage, retention and disposal of records
  • understood that you must keep all documents about assessments, including invoices and the assessors’ contact and professional details, for six years
  • understood DWP may request copies of assessment documents at any time within six years
  • understood that your data, for example your contact details or business information will be shared with DWP and relevant independent research agencies - to evaluate the service on behalf of DWP

Referral pathways

You agree that you have:

  • documented incoming and on-going referral procedures and case management
  • access to a doctor on the GMC specialist register for the escalation of cases

Comprehensive assessment reports

You agree that your reports will:

  • use a biopsychosocial approach
  • contain objective and impartial advice
  • address any employer or management concerns
  • cover the employee’s capacity for employment
  • advise on the current functional ability of the employee
  • include onward referrals, if required

Limitation of liability

You agree that:

  • you must accept full responsibility and liability if there is any fraud by you or your employees
  • you accept full responsibility and liability for the quality and performance of all the workplace health assessments
  • DWP cannot guarantee you will get any additional business from being part of the scheme
  • DWP is not responsible for any losses or additional costs caused by a delay outside of DWP’s control
  • DWP is only liable for paying any valid invoices you send us
  • DWP is not liable for any outcomes from your assessments or from the scheme being withdrawn, withheld or suspended
  • nothing in these terms and conditions limits or excludes our liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation
  • only you and DWP can take legal action to make sure the terms and conditions are followed. Nobody else has any rights under these terms and conditions
  • you cannot transfer your rights and responsibilities of the scheme to another provider

After assessments

You understand that:

  • the employer and employee can ask follow-up questions about the report
  • follow-up questions can be billed as part of the original assessment or as a new assessment
  • if a new assessment is needed, you will need to get another discount code from the employer
  • you can only invoice DWP and the employer after you complete the assessment

Removal from the scheme

You agree and understand that:

  • if you do not work within the terms and conditions DWP will remove you from the scheme and may refuse or delay payment
  • DWP may change the scheme, the eligibility requirements and terms and conditions at any time
  • if you are no longer eligible for the scheme DWP can remove you from the scheme and may refuse or delay payment. This includes if DWP changes its eligibility requirements
  • DWP can close or suspend the scheme at any time and may refuse payments following its closure or suspension
  • you cannot appeal a decision by DWP if we decide you are not eligible to join the scheme or if we remove you from the provider register
  • if you, your employees, or subcontractors provide inaccurate information or collude with SMEs using the scheme, DWP may recover any money incorrectly paid to you and may remove you from the register
  • you will repay any incorrect payments from DWP within 14 days of DWP’s request - if you do not, you will pay an 8% annual interest on that amount
  • DWP may temporarily suspend your registration and investigate before we decide to remove you from the register, and you can make representations to us before this happens
  • if DWP decides to remove you from the register, we will contact you to let you know
  • your registration is suspended or cancelled, or if you withdraw your registration, you will not provide any assessments as part of the scheme

Consent and cancellations

If an employee withdraws consent after an assessment

Follow your own policy for when an employee withdraws consent after an assessment. You can still invoice DWP for 80% of the cost and the employer for 20%.

If an employee cancels or does not attend an assessment

You cannot charge DWP if an employee cancels or does not attend an assessment. Use your cancellation and non-attendance policy for any charges to the employer.

If an employer does not pay for an assessment

DWP will still pay 80% of the cost of any completed assessment. We will not pay for any financial losses if the employer does not pay.
