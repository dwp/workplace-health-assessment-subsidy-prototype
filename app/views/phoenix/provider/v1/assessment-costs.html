Assessment costs

For this scheme, the occupational health assessment should cost no more than £400.

If there are travel expenses, they should be included in this cost. DWP will pay 80% of the £400 cost, which will be capped at £320. The employer will pay 20% up to the value of £80.

If the costs exceed this amount, for example due to travel cost or if the assessment requires a doctor, you must make sure the employer is aware of this and agrees to pay anything above the £80 in full.

VAT is not usually applied to an occupational health assessment if it is carried out by a registered healthcare professional.

You must charge your usual price for assessments. You cannot charge for supplying the quote.

If you get accepted onto the scheme, you will need to provide the price range of your assessments. The costs may be different depending on the:

  • complexity of the case
  • type of health care professional that does the assessment
  • assessment method

Examples of costs

Remote assessment with a nurse

Sam has a video call assessment with a nurse. It costs £150. You invoice his employer for £30 and DWP for £120.

In-person assessment with an occupational therapist

Kat has an in-person assessment with an occupational therapist. The assessment costs £240. You invoice his employer for £48 and DWP for £192.
