How it works

The Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) and the Department of Health and Social Care (DHSC) are trialling a new workplace health assessment discount scheme.

It is called ‘Get a workplace health assessment to support an employee’s health and disability’.

The aim is to help small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) find workplace health providers to carry out occupational health assessments for their employees.

A pilot has already started for this scheme and is expected to run for about 16 weeks.

Which employers can use the scheme

The scheme is open to SMEs registered in England or Wales which have 250 employees or less.

  • yearly turnover is £10.2 million or less
  • their yearly balance sheet is £5.1 million or less

Eligible SMEs can use the scheme to:

  • get a code for an 80% discount on workplace health assessments for their employees
  • find a private sector workplace health provider to do the assessment

Provider register

If you are an eligible workplace health provider, you can apply to deliver assessments using this scheme.

Your details will be added to the public provider register.

Employers will be able to search and choose a provider to carry out the occupational health assessments using this register.
