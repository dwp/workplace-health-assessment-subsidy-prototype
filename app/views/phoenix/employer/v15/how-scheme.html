How to use the discount code

  1. Check if your business is eligible and get a discount code.
  2. Search the list of providers and book an assessment.
  3. Contact a provider to book an assessment.
  4. Give the provider your discount code.
  5. After the assessment, you will get advice about free or funded onward support for your employee.
  6. Pay the provider when they invoice you.
  7. DWP will pay 80% of the cost of the assessment.

If you need another discount code

You can use this scheme for all your employees, including yourself.

Employees can have more assessments for ongoing or new cases.

For every assessment, you will need to get a new discount code by answering the eligibility questions again.

Discount codes do not expire.


What an assessment will cost

An assessment will cost between £20 and £80 after the 80% discount.

For this scheme, the occupational health assessment should cost no more than £400.

DWP will pay 80% of the £400 cost, which will be capped at £320.

You will pay 20% up to the value of £80.If the costs exceed this amount for example, because of travel costs, or the cost of the assessment, you will have to pay anything that is above the £80, in full, to the provider.

VAT is not usually applied to an occupational health assessment if it is carried out by a registered healthcare professional.


Why the cost of assessments differ

The cost can be different depending on:

  • the complexity of the case
  • the type of health care professional that does the assessment

Examples

Sam has a video call assessment with a nurse. It costs £150, so his employer pays £30.

Emma has an in-person assessmen video call with an occupational therapist It costs £250, so her employer pays £50.


If an employee needs to cancel or reschedule an assessment

Cancelling or reschedulling an assessment might result in a fee.

You need to check with the workplace health provider what their cancellation policy is.


If an employee does not attend an assessment

You will have to pay for the full cost of an assessment if your employee does not attend. You will have to pay any cancellation or rescheduling fees in full.

Reassuring and reminding your employee about the assessment process means they are more likely to attend.
